Project Food

Locker

By: The Wall-ees

Front line workers have been proven to be at higher risk of exposure to COVID. Some of the most vulnerable are fast food or restaurant workers that deal with a higher number of people daily and are most often forget or not treated as a priority. Nearly a quarter of fast-food workers contracted COVID-19 in the last eighteen months, and less than half were notified by their employers after they had been exposed to COVID-19.

Project Overview

We decided to focus on this group of front line workers by creating the Food Locker which would achieve our goal of limiting the interaction of workers with customers. The Food Locker will feature motorized doors, displays, insulated inside, UV light cleaning, and smart autonomous features that limit the touching of surfaces to a minimum.

  • When an order is ready the worker will place the order in one of the empty spaces and input the order number. When the customer wants to pick up their order they have to input their order number and the front door will automatically open and close for them.
  • We want a responsive system so we want a short lag between inputting the code and the door opening. As a result we want the lag to be less than half a second. The lock will need to be a fraction faster to retract the locking mechanism.
  • Our performance will be the overall speed of the system i.e. how fast the customer can take out their order. In order to do this our system needs to have a solid microcontroller and a linear actuator with proper timing.
  • The lockers will need to be properly insulated so that hot items remain hot and cold items remain cold.
  • The system should minimize contact between the locker and the user to preserve cleanliness and help prevent the spread of Covid and other bacteria/viruses.
