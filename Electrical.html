Comparator and Wheatstone Bridge

The output voltage will be compared to a constant reference voltage from the other voltage divider in the Wheatstone bridge without a variable resistance. Comparator gives us the ability to check the value of the FSRs(have a load or not) and Photoresistors(which side RFID card is being scanned).

Rx= Variable resistance i.e: FSRs and Photoresistors

  • –
    The behavior of Photoresistors indifferently lit environments will be used to approximate the additional resistances in the resistor bridge. The input of photoresistors changes depending on the environment, hysteresis allows the circuit for photoresistors to stay on or off even when there is a change in the environment.
  • –
    The behavior of FSRs with different loads will be used to approximate the additional resistances in the resistor bridge.

FSR Application:
Two FSRs are going to be used in the 2 separate compartments of the food locker to detect if there is a load in either compartment. This will be used for the Arduino to know when the automatically close a door, turn on cleaning LEDs and Indicate if the food locker is loaded or empty.

Photo Resistor application:
Two photoresistors will be used to determine which side of the food locker an individual is scanning their RFID card. This is done so that the desired door is opened regardless of the number of times the RFID card is swiped. Additionally, LEDs will be used to indicate on which side the door will open.

Indicating LEDs[RED] → ~1.8V<Vdrop<~2.2V → Iforward= 20mA → R(Red)=220 ohm

Photoresistor and laser used to close door

A laser and photoresistor will be used to close the doors. The laser will be constantly on towards the photoresistor. When the laser is cut off by waving a hand across it the photoresistor will detect that, indicating that the door will be closed.
